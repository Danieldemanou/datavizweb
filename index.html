2015 : THE START OF THE BIGGEST REFUGEE CRISIS

Introduction

This work has been done by 3 students from Télécom Paris Tech and Sciences Po Paris.
Our goal is to show the explosion of refugees from 1999 to 2016 which increased by 62% with a special focus on the Syrian refugee crisis. It aims to show the world that the Syrian refugee crisis is by far the biggest crisis the world has experienced in the last decade with more than 5 millions Syrian refugees since the beginning of the crisis . You will see a map that helps you visualize the movement of refugee from 1999 to 2016 and the crisis associated. Then, we will focus on the Syrian crisis to make you realize the humanitarian disaster behind.

REFUGEES MIGRATION FROM 1999 TO THE BIG CRISIS

The Syrian Civil War started in 2011, but it is in 2014 that the first waves of Syrian refugees began.
In 2015, as the war intensifies with strong represion from the Assad’s regime and the takeover of big cities from ISIS,
the Syrian refugee crisis reach its peak.

THE HUMANS BEHIND

Children. Women. Men. These are the people that has escaped the Syrian horror.
Each of them has a story, a name, a life, a love story, feelings, an age, everything that makes up LIFE.

Refugees struggle with language barriers, finding work,
and meeting people in their temporary new homes.

Without a working knowledge of French, many struggle to find employment outside of the refugee community itself. As expected, children develop language skills quickly.
But with adults the barriers of language easily become barriers of understanding.
Therefore language is the primary obstacle which divides the refugee from the rest of its citizens.Given the divide which language and politics can pose, images and stories have become the solution to bridging the gap between people. Photos, such as that of Omran Daqneesh, the Syrian boy who became the face of the refugee crisis, remind us that refugees are people not statistics
